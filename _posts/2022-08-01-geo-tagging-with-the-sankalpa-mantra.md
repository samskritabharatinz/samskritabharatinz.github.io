If you have ever attended or participated in a traditional Hindu ritual, chances are you have heard the Sankalpa Mantra. A Sankalpa, or a determination to perform the ritual, is a prelude to a ritual. This mantra is also a fine example of geotagging from Vedic times, as it describes the precise time and place starting from Brahma's life.

## The Sankalpa Mantra

As it is date and place dependent, some relevant data needs to be gathered before commencing. For example, if you were to do a puja today, the Sankalpa Mantra would run as follows (abridged):

> Mama Upaatta Samastaduritakshayadwaara Shri Parameshwara Preetyartham Shube Shobhane Muhurte Om Vishnuh Vishnuh Vishnuh. Adya Brahmanah Dwitiya Parardhe Sri Shvetavarahakalpe Vaivasvatamanvantare Ashtavimshatitame Kaliyuge Prathame Pade Bhurloke Jambudvipe Bharatavarshe Bharatakhande **New Zealand Kshetre Wellington Nagare** Mero Dakshine Parshwe ... Shubha Tithou.

Then one goes on to describe their lineage - their Gotra, Name, Star and so on.

So what does this signify? Let us analyse it word by word:

- **मम उपात्त समस्तदुरितक्षयद्वारा** - For removing all problems and pains in life
- **श्रीपरमेश्वरप्रीत्यर्थं** - For making the Lord Parameshwara happy
- **शुभे शोभने मुहूर्ते** - This auspicious time
- **अद्यब्रह्मणः** - Beginning from Brahma's life
- **द्वितीयपरार्धे** - We are now in the 2nd Parardha (half) of Brahma's life
- **श्रीश्वेतवराहकल्पे** - In Shweta Varaha Kalpa
- **वैवस्वतमन्वन्तरे** - In the reigning period of the current manu Vivasvaan, hence Vaivasvata
- **अष्टाविंशतितमे** - The 28th mahayuga of the current manvantara
- **कलियुगे** - We are in Kali Yuga
- **प्रथमे पादे** - In the first quarter of this yuga
- **भूर्लोके** - On Earth
- **जम्बूद्वीपे** - The place where the ritual is performed. India was known as Jambudweepa
- **भारतवर्षे भरतखण्डे** - In the land of Bhaarat
- **न्यूज़ीलैंड क्षेत्रे** - In New Zealand
- **वेलिङ्टन् नगरे** - In Wellington City
- **मेरोः दक्षिणे पार्श्वे** - To the south of the Meru mountain
- **शुभकृत नाम संवत्सरे** - This year (Samvatsara) is named Shubakrith
- **दक्षिणायन अयने** - The southward half of the year (Dakshinaayana)
- **शिशिर ऋतौ** - The season (of the six ritus), here Shishira (Winter)
- **श्रावण मासे** - The month
- **कृष्ण पक्षे** - The waning fortnight of the lunar month
- **अमावास्य शुभतिथौ** - The tithi, here Amavasya
- **पुष्य नक्षत्रयुक्तायां** - The star of the day
- **गुरु वासरयुक्तायां** - Thursday

## So what does this mean?

This is geo-tagging from the beginning of Brahma's life. From a brief analysis of the Sankalpa mantra we can infer:

- We are in the first day (Shveta Varaha Kalpa). One Kalpa is 4,320,000,000 years, or 4.32 billion years.
- We are in the 28th Chatur Yuga, and each of them is about 4,320,000 years.

Thus we can conclude we are just a little over 4.4 billion years into this day of Brahma. Modern science has calculated the age of the earth to be around 4.5 billion years.

In fact, the scriptures go further into this cycle and estimate the age of the Universe to be about 155.5 trillion years, based on the fact that we are in the 51st year of Brahma.

Isn't it amazing that such big numbers were discussed thousands of years ago in ancient Sanskrit works? In my next blog, we will delve into the other end of the timescale and talk about really small numbers.
